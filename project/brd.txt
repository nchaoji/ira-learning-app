1. Summary
Ira is a Class 11 student (CBSE, PCB + Psychology) preparing simultaneously for CBSE boards, CUET, university-specific entrance exams (Christ, Ashoka, TISS), German language proficiency at Goethe-Institut, and NIOS Mathematics — seven parallel preparation tracks over 22 months.
The primary risk is not capability, but drift — the gradual erosion of consistency across multiple tracks when no single track has an immediate deadline. A week of skipped GK becomes a month. A paused German course becomes a dropped one. The compound effect of drift across seven tracks is what derails even well-planned strategies.
This app is a Learning Intelligence Platform that prevents drift, ensures retention through neuroscience-based reinforcement methods, makes progress visible to both Ira and her parent, and sustains motivation over a 22-month horizon through gamification mechanics that match her engagement patterns.

The governing principle
Make learning effective, not just tracked. The app doesn’t just record that she studied — it ensures she remembers what she studied, identifies gaps before they become problems, and connects daily effort to long-term outcomes. 
Create knowledge (learning sessions), 
Capture it (active recall and brain dumps), 
Convert it (spaced repetition into durable memory).

Design principle: trust, not surveillance
The parent dashboard shows aggregated progress, not real-time activity monitoring. Niranjan sees track health (green/amber/red), streak status, and milestone progress — not every brain dump or journal entry. This respects Ira’s autonomy while keeping the parent informed. Trust but verify.
2. Objectives
2.1 Primary Objectives
Prevent preparation drift across 7 parallel tracks over 22 months by making daily engagement habitual and visible.
Maximize retention of learned material through neuroscience-based active recall and spaced repetition, ensuring that what she learns in Aakash, Goethe, and self-study actually stays in long-term memory.
Provide real-time progress visibility to both Ira (motivational, achievement-focused) and her parent (strategic, exception-based), enabling timely course correction.
Build exam readiness progressively, so that by the time boards and entrance exams arrive, she has data-driven confidence in her preparation rather than anxiety-driven cramming.
2.2 Secondary Objectives
Build the profile elements (reading, writing, volunteering) that differentiate her applications for Ashoka, Christ, and TISS — not as checkbox activities but as trackable, compounding habits.
Create a structured record of her intellectual journey (journal entries, book reflections, concept maps) that directly feeds application essays and interview preparation.
Sustain the Germany option by tracking German language progression against the required timeline for Studienkolleg readiness.

6. Functional Requirements
6.1 Curriculum &amp; Learning Path Management
The app must know what Ira is supposed to learn, structured hierarchically.
FR-6.1.1: Curriculum Structure
Four-level hierarchy: Track → Module → Chapter → Concept.
Example: PCB-Biology → Human Physiology → Digestion &amp; Absorption → Enzymes of the Small Intestine.
Each concept has a status: Not Started, Covered, Recall-Weak (red), Recall-Fading (amber), Recall-Strong (green).
FR-6.1.2: Curriculum Data Entry
Manual entry interface: parent or Ira can add subjects, modules, chapters, and concepts.
Bulk upload via structured template (CSV or JSON).
Progressive enrichment: start with chapter-level, add concept-level detail as each chapter is covered.
FR-6.1.3: Learning Path Timeline
Each track has a target completion date (e.g., Physics syllabus complete by Feb 2028).
The app calculates required pace (chapters per week) and tracks actual vs required progress.
Visual indicator: “X weeks ahead” or “X weeks behind schedule.”
6.2 Daily Dashboard &amp; Task Engine
FR-6.2.1: Ira’s Daily View
Shows today’s date and a motivational streak summary (GK streak, recall streak, overall level).
Surfaces 3–5 contextual tasks based on the day: spaced reviews due, GK quiz, post-class recall (if Aakash day), journal prompt.
Tasks are prioritised: overdue spaced reviews first, then today’s core tasks, then optional enrichment.
Completion of each task awards XP with a brief animation.
FR-6.2.2: Calendar Awareness
Aakash class schedule input (which days, which subjects). The app knows “today is a Physics day” and triggers the relevant recall session.
Goethe class schedule input. Triggers German recall on class days.
School schedule awareness for Psychology class days.
Exam countdown timers: boards, CUET, Christ entrance, Ashoka deadline.
6.3 Post-Class Recall &amp; Active Retrieval System
The core learning engine. Based on neuroscience principles: active recall, generation effect, elaborative interrogation, and interleaving.
FR-6.3.1: Brain Dump
Triggered after each Aakash/Goethe/school Psychology class.
Blank text area with prompt: “Write everything you remember from today’s class. No notes. Pure recall.”
Timer shown (suggested 5–10 minutes). Word count tracked.
Content is saved and linked to the relevant track, module, and date.
FR-6.3.2: The Three Questions
After brain dump, three sequential prompts:
“What was the hardest concept today?” (free text)
“Explain it in your own words as if teaching a friend.” (free text, generation effect)
“What’s one question you still have?” (free text, open loop / Zeigarnik effect)
FR-6.3.3: Connect Prompt
Final prompt: “How does anything from today connect to something from another subject?”
This forces interleaving and cross-subject elaboration.
Optional — can be skipped, but skipping forfeits bonus XP.
FR-6.3.4: Session Completion
Self-rating: “How much could you remember today? 1–5.”
XP awarded. Recall streak updated.
Content automatically queued for spaced repetition (see 6.4).
6.4 Spaced Repetition Engine
FR-6.4.1: Scheduling Algorithm
After each recall session, the app schedules review prompts at: Day 1 (next day), Day 2 (2 days later), Day 4, Day 7, Day 14.
Each review asks: “Can you recall [concept/hardest topic] from [date]?”
If recall is successful (self-rated 4–5), interval extends. If unsuccessful (1–3), interval resets to Day 1.
After 4 successful consecutive recalls, the concept is marked “strong” (green) and enters a maintenance cycle (monthly check).
FR-6.4.2: Review Interface
Shows the concept or topic. Does NOT show the original brain dump initially.
Ira writes her recall attempt.
After writing, she can optionally view her original brain dump to compare and self-assess.
Self-rates recall quality (1–5).
FR-6.4.3: Queue Management
Daily dashboard shows number of reviews due.
Overdue reviews are flagged and prioritised.
Maximum reviews per day: configurable (default 10) to prevent overwhelm.
6.5 Progress Tracking &amp; Analytics
FR-6.5.1: Syllabus Progress
Per track: percentage of curriculum covered, chapters completed vs total.
Pace indicator: weeks ahead / on track / weeks behind vs planned schedule.
Visual: progress bar or journey map per track.
FR-6.5.2: Concept Mastery Map
Visual heat map of all concepts across a subject.
Green = strong recall (3+ successful retrievals). Amber = fading (1–2 retrievals or declining scores). Red = weak (failed recall or never reviewed).
Filterable by subject, module, chapter.
FR-6.5.3: Exam Readiness Score
Per subject: percentage of concepts at “strong” recall.
Becomes prominent from 6 months before boards (September 2027).
Target benchmark: 70%+ green by 3 months before boards.
FR-6.5.4: Trend Charts
Recall quality scores over time (weekly rolling average, per subject).
Brain dump word count trend (proxy for recall volume).
Spaced repetition completion rate over time.
GK category coverage distribution.
6.6 Gamification System
Designed to match engagement patterns observed in Subway Surfer (endless streaks, quick dopamine, near-miss tension) and Roblox (achievement collection, visible progression, identity).
FR-6.6.1: XP (Experience Points)
Earned for: completing daily GK (+10), recall sessions (+25), spaced reviews (+15), journal entries (+5), weekly synthesis (+50), volunteering log (+20), book completion (+100).
XP drives level progression.
FR-6.6.2: Levels
Themed progression: Explorer (L1–3) → Scholar (L4–6) → Navigator (L7–9) → Pathfinder (L10–12) → Trailblazer (L13+).
Level up triggers a celebratory animation and unlocks new badge slots.
FR-6.6.3: Streaks
Per-track streaks: GK daily streak, recall session streak (per Aakash day), German class attendance streak, writing streak (weekly).
Master streak: consecutive days with at least one meaningful activity across any track.
Streak milestones: 7, 14, 30, 50, 100, 200 days. Each milestone = achievement badge.
Streak freeze: 1 free freeze per 30 days (like Duolingo). Preserves streak during illness or travel. Cannot be stacked.
FR-6.6.4: Achievement Badges
Milestone badges: “First 30-Day GK Streak,” “A1 German Complete,” “First Psychology Book Finished,” “100 Recall Sessions,” “50 Concepts Mastered (Green),” “First Volunteering Session Logged.”
Subject mastery badges: unlocked when 80%+ of concepts in a module are green.
Special badges: “Cross-Subject Connector” (10 connect prompts completed), “Weekly Synthesiser” (4 consecutive weekly synthesis challenges).
Badges displayed on profile. Collection view.
FR-6.6.5: Near-Miss Nudges
When a streak is at risk (approaching end of day with no activity): “Your 47-day GK streak ends in 2 hours! Quick quiz to keep it alive?”
This is the Subway Surfer near-miss tension — the emotional cost of breaking a long streak is a powerful motivator.

6.7 Nudge &amp; Notification Engine
FR-6.7.1: Smart Notifications
Streak protection: alerts when a streak is about to break.
Spaced repetition reminders: “You have 3 reviews due today.”
Post-class triggers: “Aakash Physics class done? Time for recall.” (based on calendar-aware schedule)
Milestone approach: “Goethe A1 course ends in 2 weeks — exam registration reminder.”
Progress celebrations: “You’ve mastered 50 concepts this month! That’s 12 more than last month.”
FR-6.7.2: Contextual Nudges
Connection nudges: “Your GK quiz today connects to your TISS BAT prep — social awareness is 30% of the BAT.”
Gap nudges: “You haven’t logged volunteering in 3 weeks. Even 2 hours this weekend helps your Ashoka profile.”
Reinforcement nudges: “Your Biology recall scores have been above 4/5 for 3 weeks. The method is working!”
FR-6.7.3: Notification Controls
Ira can configure quiet hours (e.g., during Aakash class, during sleep).
Maximum notifications per day: configurable (default: 5).
Exam-season mode: reduces non-essential notifications, focuses on recall and review only.
6.8 Journal &amp; Reflection Module
FR-6.8.1: Free-Form Journal
Text entry with optional tags: #psychology, #bookreflection, #currentaffairs, #personal, #volunteering.
Entries are private to Ira (parent dashboard does not show content, only frequency).
Word count tracked. Writing streak maintained.
FR-6.8.2: Prompted Reflections
Weekly reflection prompt: “What’s one thing you learned this week that changed how you think about something?”
Post-book prompt: “You finished [book title]. What was the single most surprising idea?”
Monthly prompt: “Look back at your journal entries this month. What patterns do you notice in what interests you?”
FR-6.8.3: Application Essay Feed
When Ashoka/Christ application season approaches, the app surfaces a curated feed of her best journal entries, book reflections, and volunteering logs as raw material for essays.
This is not AI-generated essay content — it’s her own words and reflections, organised for easy reference.
6.9 Parent Dashboard
FR-6.9.1: Track Health Overview
7 tracks displayed as green/amber/red status cards.
Green: streak active, progress on schedule. Amber: streak broken or progress slipping (1–2 weeks behind). Red: no activity for 2+ weeks or significantly behind schedule.
One-glance design: the parent should assess overall health in under 30 seconds.
FR-6.9.2: Weekly Summary
Auto-generated summary: activities completed, streaks maintained or broken, notable achievements, any tracks that moved from green to amber or red.
Delivered as an in-app view and optionally as a weekly email/notification.
FR-6.9.3: Milestone Tracker
The 22-month timeline from the roadmap document, displayed as a visual timeline.
Each milestone (A1 German by Dec 2026, NIOS Maths registration by Oct 2027, Ashoka application by Jan 2028, etc.) shown with status: upcoming / on track / at risk / completed.
FR-6.9.4: Exam Readiness View
Available from ~6 months before boards.
Per-subject concept mastery distribution (green/amber/red percentages).
Projected readiness based on current trends.
FR-6.9.5: Data the Parent Does NOT See
Content of brain dumps and journal entries (private to Ira).
Real-time activity feed (no live tracking).
Individual recall session details (only aggregated scores and trends).

8. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance: app loads in under 2 seconds on a standard mobile connection. Recall session interface is lag-free for text input.
Availability: 99%+ uptime. This is a daily-use habit app — downtime breaks streaks and erodes trust.
Data privacy: Ira’s brain dump and journal content is private. Parent dashboard shows aggregated metrics only. All data encrypted at rest and in transit.
Mobile-first: designed for mobile viewport (375–430px width). Desktop access for parent dashboard is secondary.
PWA installability: installable on Android and iOS via browser. Service worker for basic offline capability (queue brain dumps offline, sync when connected).
Data persistence: server-side database with user authentication. Data survives app reinstalls and phone changes.
Notification reliability: push notifications must be reliable — streak protection and spaced repetition reminders are time-sensitive.

Why phase this way
Phase 1 ships fast (within 6 weeks) and gets the core habit loop running: do the task → earn XP → maintain streak → see progress. The more sophisticated features (spaced repetition, analytics, AI) layer on top once the daily habit is established. You can’t optimise retention if she isn’t using the app daily — so engagement comes first, intelligence comes second.
